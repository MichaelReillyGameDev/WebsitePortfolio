Welcome!

My Name is Michael Reilly

Currently, I am a QA Analyst at Team17 and a Game Designer by study. I have a BSc degree in Computer Gaming Technology from Anglia Ruskin University and was employed at Polyfruit Studios LTD as a Game Designer working on Hyper-Casual mobile games before working at Team17. On this page, you will find a portfolio of my work and my professional experience. Please scroll down to view my entire portfolio or press the button below to see my self-published games.

Self-Published Games

Commercial Releases

Team17 - QA Analyst

September 2022 - Present

Killer Frequency

September 2022 - April 2023

Store Link

When testing Killer Frequency, I had first-hand experience with how game development works on a scale as grand as Team17 and how important quality assurance is to game development and how often communicates with both production and design to create the best possible product.

Some of the things I have learned working on Killer Frequency are: how to set up console test kits with their development software and to install builds; how to log game bugs clearly and effectively; execute scripted test plans; the importance of constant key communication with the test lead and the rest of the team; and how to work efficiently during an intense submission period.


Headbangers: Rythm Royale

May 2023 - Present

Store Link

My time on Headbangers Rythm Royale was spent to perfect the QA skills I had been introduced to by working on Killer Frequency, with the addition on taking the challenges that come with testing a multiplayer title.

Working on a multipler title, bugs with a low reproduce rates, edgecase issues, and bugs that require a long setup would be more frequent. This meant that my reports and evidence needed to be more descriptive than ever so the other departments could get the issues resolved as soon as possible.

Polyfruit Studios - Game Designer

May 2021 - November 2021

Aqwa

September 2022 - April 2023

Store Link

Aqwa is a mobile game that I worked on as the primary designer in Polyfruit Studios LTD. The project was worked on during April and June 2021.

As the game designer for Aqwa, it was my responsibility to develop the game using Unity. To achieve a finished product I would use my leadership skills to liase with the Art, Programming and QA teams highlighting what I would need while providing constructive feedback while applying any that I had recieved myself.

Solo Project Work

Parking Paradise

Parking Paradise was a vertical slice of a potential solo commercial game I created as a way to maintain my game development skills while looking for employment post-university.

The game is something I am passionate about as a concept; as is, it could be pushed very far and provide an experience that has not been done in games.

Parking Paradise's development ceased in May 2023.
