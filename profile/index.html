About me

Always looking for new challenges, I am interested in computer games and hope to continue working in the games industry. My background is in both QA and Design on a junior level, but I am pushing for more senior roles. I consider myself self-sufficient and self-motivated to continue learning and developing my skills.

Design Skills

My toolbelt in design always expands; I have job experience as a game designer and many years of experience developing my design skills.

While my background in design is in computer games, I believe my talents are versatile enough to adapt to other areas of digital design, such as graphic design or web design.

Below is a list of design-related software I have been practising for many years:

  • Photoshop and Illustrator

  • Video editing including Premiere

  • Unity and Unreal Engine

As well as software, I am capable of writing documentation for my design work.

Development Skills

With my natural desire to explore and learn with my passion for technology and DIY attitude. I have entered my design and QA roles with a technological mind which has been used as an advantage for efficiency and figuring out how to solve issues quickly, dig deeper into my roles and assist my peers.

As a game designer with Unity not only am I capable of visual scripting, but I am able to code games in C# which is also my primary way of creating games.

QA Skills

I have a year of industry experience in the QA Department for the well-known publisher Team17. During my tenure, I tested and released several games with my name in the credits of two games.

I can confidently set up and install builds for every eighth and ninth-generation console’s dev kits, conduct and accurately follow test plans and provide solid exploratory/destructive testing. 

In my year at QA, I had successfully created hundreds of detailed bug reports and regressed just as many.
